The Software Engineer's View of the Future:

  • Eventually the user interface is a virtual reality scene.
  • The user may discuss their needs and desires with a bot in the scene.
  • The bot interprets requests and translates those to application commands.
  • Application commands are routed to SimpleEnglish framework.
  • The framework takes the commands as goals and creates plans to meet the goals.
  • The SimpleEnglish task-master executes the resulting plan.
  • The task-master informs the communication-actor (com-actor) as the plan steps are executed.
  • The com-actor (UI) can provide feedback as needed to the user.
    If the user is still in the virtual reality scene, the feedback could be provided using scene action metaphors or simply communicated verbally by a bot.

Programming:

  • User Story: The user story describes the interactions between the parties.
    Each sentence describes a dialog. The dialogs will be programmed in a dialog language. (Perhaps a SimpleEnglish re-styling of AIML augmented with SimpleTemplate.) 

    ---
    Story: Customer purchases items from our on-line store.
    . Customer is welcomed to store.
    . Customer is directed to areas of interest.
    . Customer selects potential purchases into cart.
    . Customer edits cart content.
    . Customer purchases items in cart.
    . We ship purchased items to customer.
    . We notify customer of shipment.
    . We obtain payment from customer's payment agency.
    Exceptions:
    . Customer abandons cart.
    . We have an out-of-stock item.
    . Customer payment credentials fail on re-validation.
    ---

  • Test Plan: The user story forms a high-level test plan.
  • Business Process: Business processes are described by a plan-base
    containing task-frames that describe individual processes. 
    The goal: "We ship purchased items to customer."
    matches the title of a task frame
    that describes the end-game moves in the business process.
    ---
    Task: We ship purchased items to customer.
    Post: Purchased items are shipped to customer.
    Pre: 
    . Customer accepts sales agreement.
    . . (with shipping arrangement and payment terms)
    . Purchased items are packaged for shipment.
    Steps:
    . Transfer shipping package to shipping company.
    . Notify customer that purchased items are shipped.
    . Debit the customer's account for items shipped.
    ---

  • The task-master creates a plan by back-chaining from this task-frame
    to the other task-frames indicated by the pre-conditions.
    ---
    Task: Customer accepts sales agreement.
    Post: Sales agreement is accepted by customer.
    Pre: 
    . Customer selects shipping arrangements.
    . Customer provides payment credentials.
    Steps:
    . Present complete sales agreement.
    . Ask customer to confirm acceptance of sales agreement.
    ---
    Task: Package items in order for shipment.
    Post: Purchased items are packaged for shipment.
    Pre: 
    . Items in the order are all in the packing department.
    Steps:
    . Get appropriate shipping container. (suggested size box)
    . Move items from warehouse basket to shipping container.
    . Fill remaining space with padding.
    . Seal box.
    . Attach shipping labels.
    ---
    Task: Pick items in the order.
    Post: Items in the order are all in the packing department.
    Pre:
    . Picker has pick-list for a customer order.
    Steps:
    . Get warehouse basket from basket queue.
    . Travel the pick-list and pull items from shelves into basket.
    . Move basket to the packing department.
    --- 
    Task: Provide pick-list for order.
    Post: Picker has pick-list for a customer order.
    Pre:
    . Sales agreement is accepted by customer.
    . Customer payment credentials are re-validated.
    Steps:
    . Compute best route for picking.
    . Generate the pick-list.
    . Deliver the pick-list to next available picker.
    ---

The Operating Environment

  • Run-Time System: The framework simply communicates each action in the task-plan to an actor, a software actor. The actor, in turn, may communicate to other actors or a robot or a human worker. When instructions are given to a human worker, a task-appropriate human interface is used. The human interface could be a sheet of paper, a mobile display, an interactive voice response system, or a virtual reality display with 3D metophors.
    - The system may be highly distributed involving many actors hosted on geographically dispersed hosts. Coordination is all asynchronous using secure messaging. The messages contain SimpleEnglish sentences.
  • Accounting: Actors are using resources and moving assets around. Accounting is built-into the statement-understanding and process instrumentation. The general ledger operates in real-time.
  • Memory and History:  Programmers will delegate record-keeping to the framework. They will no longer need to spend time assuring that the recording system is functioning. Agents have only a small amount of current data to remember - very simple persistance systems will handle that. Businesses will use off-the-shelf "appliance" systems to record and retrieve facts about customers, suppliers, resources, and accounts.
  • Component Architecture: The essential components of the system include:
    • Human Interface Agent - communicates with human clients and workers
    • Accounting Service - operates accounting services, general ledger
    • Contact Database - tracks people and parties
    • Plan KnowledgeBase - stores the business process representation
    • Task Master - runs each task to completion or termination
    • Plan Maker - builds a plan for each goal
    • Resource Pool (class) - supertype for all resource pools: allocates, assigns, tracks
    • Resource (class) - supertype for all resources
    • Actor (class) - a resource that can perform action statements
    • Utilization db - hold resource utilization statistics, facilitates Monte Carlo draws.

Consequences:

  • Business Strategy: Business process excellence will not distinguish most competitors. The buying experience is already the thing that differentiates brands in the consumer's minds. Branding will merge with the customer experience. The customer experience will occur in the virtual reality store-front.  This means that the ability to create satisfying experiences, perhaps with some entertainment thrown in, will be essential to building brand loyalty.
  • Strategic advantage will be based on understanding customer preferences and being able to provide customers with a delightful experience in addition to the old strategies concerning market position, supply logistics, and the ability to predict and respond to changes.
  • Knowledge Economy: Most business processes are not unique.  Warehouse and retail operations are well understood. The generic top-level business processes for retail operations and shipping and transportation will all be defined in a the first few months after the SimpleEnglish framework is released.
    - The more critical knowledge will be an understanding of customer preferences. In markets where the customers are young people, preferences will be relatively volatile. One might expect that services that can profile consumers in a way that allows bots to customize their behaviors might be highly valued.
  • Talent Economy: Initially the critical new knowledge will be the ability to continuously provide satisfying buyer experiences. This means creating interesting scenes and responsive bots. It appears that this will require far more artistic talent and perhaps somewhat less technical talent than has been the case in the past.
    - Currently, the work of creating highly detailed 3D scenery is very labor intensive. One might therefore expect much of this work to flow to places where labor is less expensive.
    - The work of creating good dialog requires a very good sense of local verbal idiom - including, perhaps, a good sense of the customer's cultural preferences. In the case of youth culture, this may require a good ability to follow and imitate fads. This type of work is likely to require a culturally sensitive and hence somewhat local work-force. (I am only somewhat ignoring the fact that there is a global youth culture.)
