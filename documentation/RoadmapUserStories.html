Roadmap - User Stories

Kathy was afraid that learning to program would be hard.
But learning Hum turned out to be easy.
"It is just like writing down a recipe!
Anyone can do it!"
Rosa is using Hum to design her dream house.
"I just describe the rooms
and the viewer shows me what it will look like.
I can move around and adjust the details too.
If I want to change the counter-top material,
I just touch it and say a few words."
Benny re-costumed the house greeter to look like Jeeves.
Somebody re-costumed Benny's avatar to look like Wooster.
Mom was amused.
At least the pranks had a little class.
But she restricted the virtual house scenery anyway.
Jennie learned the S-curve
for the chapter on ecology.
"Now we just need some data." she said.
Larry ran simulations
to discover the best ratios
between the smart-dust ears and eyes.
The ears detected gun-shots.
The eyes moved-in to get pictures.
"Some day this will be real." Larry said.
The stock re-ordering neural-net
was having trouble
adjusting to the big tourist events.
Robert provided it with a schedule of those events
so it would know what was causing the problem.
The simulation showed the effect of the new training.
The predicted inventory behavior looked much better.
People are installing solar generators,
switching to ground-heat,
driving hybrids unless they are telecommuting,
and the carbon market is fluctuating!
Five simultaneous trends!
What should our strategy be?
The planning team went to work on the market model
and added moves to the strategy mix.
When they ran the simulations,
some things were clear
and some things were not.
"It doesn't happen very often,"
the process owner said,
"but eventually we should automate the steps
for when the customer cancels the order."
"Can we defer that to the next release?" said the analyst.
"Yes, the existing 'freeze and step'
micro-manager feature
is a good-enough solution for now."
