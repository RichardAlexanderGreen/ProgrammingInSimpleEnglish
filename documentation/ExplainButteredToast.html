Task: Serve buttered toast.
Post: Buttered toast delivered to patron.
Pre:
. Bread is toasted.
. Butter is melted.
Actions:
. Cook: Butter the toast.
. Server: Move loaded-plate to patron's table.
---
Task: Slice the bread.
Post: Bread is sliced.
Pre:
. Baked bread is cool enough to slice.
Actions:
. Cook: Slice the bread.
---
Task: Melt the butter.
Post: Butter is melted.
Pre:
. None.
Action:
. Cook: Melt the butter.
---
Task: Toast the bread.
Post: Bread is toasted.
Pre:
. Bread is sliced.
Action:
Cook: Toast the bread.
---
Role: Cook.
Action: Toast the bread.
. Move two slices of bread to toaster.
. Set toaster to desired shade.
. Insert bread

Action: Butter the toast.
. Paint butter onto a toasted bread slice.
. Place buttered toast on a bread-plate.
. Move bread-plate to delivery counter.

Action: Slice the bread.
. Place bread loaf in slicing machine.
. Operate the slicing machine.
. Remove bread loaf.
---
Role: Server.
Action: Move loaded-plate to patron's table.
. Check loaded-plate against order.
. Move to table indicated by order.
. Deposit loaded plate near indicated patron.
---

Task frames provide a way to reduce a complicated problem to a set of simpler problems. The goal or post-condition of the task is indicated by the statement after the "Post" label. The sub-goals or pre-conditions that need to be satisfied to enable the task are listed under the "Pre" label.

The task "Serve buttered toast" cannot be worked until the bread is sliced and the butter is melted. So, we have the pre-conditions: "Bread is sliced" and "Butter is melted." Note that these pre-condition tasks can be worked in parallel.

The original goal links to two sub-goals or pre-conditions that must be satisfied first. When those sub-goals are met, we can work on the actions required to get from the pre-conditions to the post-condition. For example, to get from "Bread is sliced" and "Butter is Melted" to "Buttered toast is on the plate." The actions are: "Cook: Butter the toast" and "Server: Move loaded-plate to patron's table."

The "Butter the toast" action is assigned to a role called "Cook." The role-frame labeled "Role: Cook" defines some of the things that an actor assigned to the "Cook" role should know how to do. In this case, the role-frame includes the actions called: "Butter the toast" and "Slice the bread." Under "Deliver toast" we find a series of steps - instructions to the cook.

The final action in the task frame, "Move loaded plate to table" is assigned to the "Server" role. When the actor assigned to the server role completes that action, the original goal "Buttered toast delivered to patron" is satisfied.

Although this gets the point across, it is not a very realistic application.
